Lab-Grown Meat

Lab-grown meat, also known as cultivated or cultured meat, is genuine meat that is grown directly from animal cells; these products are "real meat, made without the need to raise and slaughter animals."

Cultivated beef may sound like a thing of the future, but it's closer to being available on supermarket shelves than you think.

In reality, the first slice of lab-grown beef made headlines in 2013 when a team at the University of Maastricht revealed the first hamburger made from bovine stem cells (Shepherd, 2022b).

What’s Remarkable About Cultivated Meat?

Ethical Benefits

  • Provides meat without slaughtering animals by growing it from animal cells.
  • ources for cells include fertilized chicken eggs, animal biopsies, and feathers.

Environmental Impact

  • Animal agriculture is a major contributor to greenhouse gas emissions, driving climate change.
  • Cultivated meat reduces the need for land and water resources compared to traditional farming methods.

Global Food Security

  • Scaled production of cultivated meat could help feed the growing world population.

How Cultivated Meat Is Made

  • Industrial Cell Culture: Similar to fermentation in brewing beer, but uses animal cells.
  • Cell Selection: Cells are taken from high-quality livestock, such as cows or chickens, for their ability to multiply and develop into meat.
  • Controlled Growth Environment: Cells are placed in sterile conditions and provided with essential nutrients to replicate and mature naturally.
  • Processing and Preparation: Once matured, the meat is harvested, processed like conventional products, and prepared for consumption.

Potential Health Advantages

  • Enhanced nutrition profiles through technology modifying amino acids, fats, vitamins, and minerals.

Vegan Considerations

  • Cultivated meat is not vegan as it originates from animal cells and involves animal by-products.
