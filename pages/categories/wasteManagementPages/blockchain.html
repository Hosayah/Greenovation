Blockchain for Waste Tracking

Among the many processes in the waste management process are segregation, transportation, recycling, disposal, and waste data analysis. These waste management procedures are impossible for governments to monitor. Governments can resolve waste management problems by using a single platform to monitor all waste management operations. That one decentralized platform might be blockchain. For a greener environment, blockchain-based waste management is assisting in tracking garbage from its production to disposal (Naveen, 2020).

Blockchain-enabled waste tracking uses decentralized ledger technology to securely, accurately, and transparently monitor and control waste disposal procedures. This system guarantees responsibility in recycling, waste collecting, and transportation by offering unchangeable records. (Insight Services Global, 2025)

How Blockchain Supports Waste Management

Rewarding Waste Segregation

  • Governments and organizations incentivize waste segregation to encourage household participation.
  • Platforms like Recerreum use blockchain and smart contracts to reward users based on the amount of segregated waste.
  • RFID-tagged bins measure waste quantity, upload data to the blockchain, and automatically issue rewards via transactions.

Real-Time Waste Tracking

  • Blockchain enables real-time tracking of waste collection, recycling, and disposal processes.
  • Platforms like Arep use Bluetooth technology to monitor waste bins and provide continuous updates on their status.
  • Helps identify challenges in waste management and improve efficiency.

Securing Data Transactions

  • Blockchain minimizes human error and ensures accurate data collection throughout the waste management process.
  • Data about waste type and quantity is uploaded to the blockchain, accessible to all stakeholders.
  • Accurate data supports informed decision-making for recycling or disposal techniques.

Promoting Sustainability

  • Encourages household participation through token-based incentives for waste segregation.
  • Improves waste management from its generation point, reducing landfill contributions.
  • Fosters a more sustainable approach to waste management, benefiting the environment.
