Green-Building/Kinetic Energy Floors

Kinetic Energy Floors

Kinetic flooring uses the scientific principle of piezoelectricity, which simply means the ability of a material to transform mechanical stress into electrical energy by harvesting the vibrational energy generated when people walk on the surface of kinetic flooring tiles. When walked on, the tiles feel similar to Astroturf and are compacted by around five millimetres. In addition to producing clean energy, the tiles are also created in a sustainable manner! Recycled tyres account for 95% of the tile content. The tiles are adaptable and can be used indoors and out (3) (Hopkins, 2024).

How Kinetic Floor Tiles Work

  • Each tile contains an electromechanical system that converts kinetic energy into electrical energy.
  • When stepped on, the tiles flex slightly, converting vertical movement into rotational movement, which powers a generator.
  • Energy output:
    • Up to 35 watts per module of sustained output.
    • 1–10 watts nominal output for continuous stepping.

Materials and Sustainability

  • Made from fully recyclable materials: glass, steel, recycled plastic, and electronics.
  • All components can be separated and recycled at the end of their lifecycle.
  • 95% of the tile contents come from recycled tires.
  • Suitable for both indoor and outdoor use.

Energy Applications

  • Generated energy can:
    • Charge phones.
    • Power streetlights, signage systems, or light up dance floors.
  • Ideal for high footfall areas such as:
    • Pavements.
    • Railway stations, airports, shopping malls, schools, and playgrounds.

Energy Efficiency

  • A single step generates 2–8 watts via electromagnetic generators beneath the tile surface.
  • Energy can be stored in lithium polymer batteries or used instantly for powering devices.

Kinetic Flooring and Data Collection

  • Integrated wireless communication technology transmits data using only 1% of generated power.
  • Tracks foot traffic and energy generated
    • Useful for optimizing energy use and placement of tiles.

These tiles not only produce clean energy but also contribute to the global renewable energy mix by capturing and utilizing otherwise wasted kinetic energy.
