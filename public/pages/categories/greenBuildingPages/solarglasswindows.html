Solar Glass Windows

Solar windows enable buildings to transform sunlight into free, renewable electricity. They operate like solar panels, turning the sun's energy into electricity that can be used. Unlike regular panels, solar glass allows light to pass through, similar to standard windows.

To make windows solar, they must have photovoltaic glazing, which includes solar cells to capture sunlight. This captured solar energy is converted into electricity that powers the electrical appliances in the building (Greenmatch, 2024).

How Solar Windows Work

  • Solar energy conversion: Solar windows are equipped with solar cells that convert sunlight into usable electricity.
  • Usage and storage: The generated electricity can power homes directly or be stored in a solar battery for later use.
  • Excess energy: Any unused energy can be supplied to the National Grid.

Efficiency of Solar Window

  • Polysolar's solar glass: Efficiency ranges between 12% to 15%, surpassing many solar panels available in the UK.

Benefits of Solar Window

  1. Free renewable electricity: Generate sustainable energy, reducing energy bills.
  2. Indoor temperature control: Reduces heat build-up during summer by limiting sunlight penetration.
  3. Ultraviolet radiation blocking: Protects furniture from fading and damage caused by UV rays.
  4. Enhanced privacy: Tinted windows provide greater privacy while maintaining clear visibility from the inside.
  5. Reduced glare: Minimizes discomfort and screen glare from sunlight.
  6. Lower installation costs: Offers a more affordable and simpler alternative to solar panel installation.
  7. Income via SEG: Allows homeowners to earn payments from energy suppliers for generating renewable electricity.
